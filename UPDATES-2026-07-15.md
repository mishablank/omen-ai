## Update 1: The Kalshi "no quotes" claim was false — dead panel revived

**Problem:** `kalshi()` read `m["yes_bid"]` / `m["yes_ask"]`. Those fields no longer exist. Every market returned `price: None`, `authed` stayed `False`, and the panel rendered "no quote" on all three rows. The [2026-07-12 session](UPDATES-2026-07-12.md) diagnosed this as an API limitation and shipped a caption telling the reader to *"add a `KALSHI_API_KEY` repo secret for authenticated cross-venue prices."*

**That diagnosis was wrong.** Kalshi's public feed exposes full quotes, depth, and last prints with **no authentication whatsoever**. The fields were renamed to `*_dollars` and are now denominated in dollars (`0.23` = 23¢), not cents — so the old code was also carrying a stale `/100.0`.

**What was done:**
- Added `kalshi_mid(m)` / `kalshi_price(m)` reading `yes_bid_dollars` / `yes_ask_dollars` / `last_price_dollars`.
- A book quoted at the bounds (bid 0 / ask 1) is **empty, not a 50¢ market** — it yields no price rather than a fake mid.
- Removed the API-key instruction from the caption; replaced with the fact that quotes are public.
- Regression test: `test_kalshi_mid_reads_dollar_denominated_fields`.

**Live result:** `authed=True`, 3/3 quoting — Recession in 2027 **41.5%**, OpenAI-or-Anthropic-IPO-first **18.5%**, US stake in both **24.0%**. No secret required, contradicting the previous session's conclusion.

## Update 2: The H100 panel's "+32% divergence" is mostly index basis, not signal

**Problem:** The panel compared Polymarket's implied month-end ($2.65) against vast.ai's realized ask median ($2.00) and concluded:

> "Market-implied month-end is **+32%** vs today's realized spot median – the market prices firmer rents than the spot tape shows."

Kalshi settles its GPU markets on the **Ornn index** (`dashboard.ornnai.com`), which prints H100 SXM at **$2.52** — while vast.ai prints **$2.00** for the same chip on the same day. vast.ai is a decentralized marketplace of interruptible hosts and sits structurally *below* a curated index. **~26% of that "+32% divergence" is the gap between two different indices.** It was being read as market conviction.

Worse for the original conclusion: on its own index, Kalshi implies month-end **$2.45 vs its $2.52 reference** — *backwardation*. Read against the right underlying, the market prices rents **softening**, not firming. The panel's conclusion was inverted.

**What was done:**
- H100 panel now shows four distinct numbers instead of two: Polymarket implied, **Kalshi implied (Ornn)**, **Ornn reference**, vast.ai ask median — each labelled with its source.
- Added an explicit **index basis** line: *"Ornn $2.52 vs vast.ai $2.00 — +26% for the same chip on the same day. Read any implied-vs-realized gap smaller than this as basis, not signal."*
- Added a **cross-venue** line (Polymarket vs Kalshi on the identical month-end) that refuses to call the spread disagreement without confirming both settle on the same index.
- Added a backwardation/contango read of Kalshi implied vs its own reference.
- Caption rewritten to state plainly that the two spot sources are different indices.

**Live result:** the panel now discloses the basis rather than charging it to sentiment.

## Update 3: Reading the Ornn index for free

**Problem:** Ornn is the settlement source for the deepest GPU-price market available, but `dashboard.ornnai.com` is auth-gated (307 redirect; no public price endpoint found at the obvious paths).

**What was done:** Kalshi's **weekly** GPU markets are directional "price to beat" contracts whose strike is set at the Ornn print when the market opens. The strike *is* the index read — free, unauthenticated, no scraping.

**Honesty constraint enforced in the code:** that strike dates to `open_time` (**2026-07-10**, five days stale), so it is labelled **"Ornn reference (week open 2026-07-10)"** and never called a live spot. The weekly's own quote tells you which way the chip moved since — H100 trades ~44% to be above $2.52 (roughly at the reference), while B200 trades **84%** above $6.69 (it has risen).

## Update 5: Quality gating — refusing to publish artifacts

**Problem:** Only H100 and A100 have tight, complete two-sided ladders. B200 and H200 monthly books are largely one-sided (bid 0 / ask 99). A naive mid over those books produced **"B200 median $3.66"** during development — a pure artifact off an empty book, ~45% below the actual reference.

**What was done:** `kalshi_ladder()` drops one-sided books and quotes wider than 15¢, then enforces **monotonicity** (survival cannot increase with strike — a higher print is a stale quote, not news). `implied_median()` interpolates the 50% crossing and returns **`None` when the crossing lies outside the quoted strikes**, rather than inventing one from the ladder edge.

The panel then **states what it cannot price and why** — "B200 1 usable strike; publishing a median off a bid-0/ask-99 book would be an artifact, so the panel shows none."

**Deliberate exclusion:** Kalshi's Dec-31 contracts (`KXH100MAX`, `KXB200MAX`) are **not** fetched. They resolve on *"above $X **by** Dec 31"* — a **running maximum**, upward-biased and not comparable to a terminal month-end value. `KXB200MAX-26DEC31` carries real size (OI 40,469), which makes it tempting and wrong. The quarterly series (`KXH100Q`, `KXB200Q`) exist with zero open markets.

**Consequence worth stating plainly:** despite the framing of the announcement, **there is no long-dated compute forward curve yet** — only two terminal points (weekly Jul 17, monthly Jul 31). Mansour's forward-curve framing is ahead of the actual liquidity. The dashboard claims no curve it cannot source.

## Update 6: The 185% probability bug

**Problem:** The valuation panel printed:

> "P(OpenAI ≥ $500B valuation): **185.0%** — euphoric zone."

`openaiHigh.reduce((s,b)=>s+b.p,0)` summed **nested cumulative** brackets: 6.5 + 11.5 + 20.5 + 26.5 + 47.5 + 72.5 = **185.0**. "Hit $600B" already contains "hit $900B"; summing double-counts. A probability cannot exceed 100%.

**What was done:** read the ≥$500B market directly — it is its own market, no arithmetic needed.

**Additional finding, surfaced not silently fixed:** the ladder is **non-monotonic** — ≥$500B at 6.5% but ≥$900B at 72.5%. Nested "hit $X" claims must *fall* as the threshold rises. This is backwards, meaning the bracket parse or the feed is wrong at a level a display fix cannot repair. Rather than quietly printing 6.5% as if it were sound, the panel now renders a **⚠ warning** that the ladder is not monotonic and the panel is unreliable until reconciled.

**This one needs a follow-up** — it is a data-layer defect, not a rendering defect, and it is out of scope of this session's fix.
