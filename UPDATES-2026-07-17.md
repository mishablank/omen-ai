# OMEN – Regime fix: no single market can trip a red "Stressed" regime

**Date:** 2026-07-17
**Files modified:** `omen/polymarket-ai-index.html`, `omen/index.html`, `omen/update-market-data.py`, `omen/test_update_market_data.py`
**Live site:** https://omen-ai.blankm.workers.dev/

---

## The problem

The monitor was showing a red **"🔴 Stressed crash regime"** while the landing page showed
**"Elevated"** — the two pages contradicted each other on the same data. The red banner was
driven by a single market: *"Chinese company has a top-3 AI model by Dec 31"* sitting at
**35.5%**, which tripped the old rule `stackMax ≥ 25% → stressed`.

Two things were wrong with that:

1. **A competition market was driving a crash warning.** "China has a top-3 model" is a
   *competitiveness* read, not distress. It says nothing about the cycle breaking.
2. **One lone market could flip the whole regime to red** even while the blended
   crash-pressure gauge was calm (26/100, well inside the Calm band). The monitor itself
   admitted this in the same breath: *"single-signal trip: blended gauge (26) is still in
   the Calm band, not broad stress."*

## The rule now

**Stressed requires a broad OR confirmed metric — never a single market on its own.**
A single market can raise **Elevated** but is capped there.

| Regime | Triggers (any one) |
|---|---|
| **Stressed** | blended gauge ≥ 55 (mean of all 6 families — broad by construction) · crash-basket average ≥ 40 (a 3-market mean) · crash-basket ≥ 25 **confirmed by** z ≥ 2 |
| **Elevated** | gauge ≥ 35 · crash-basket ≥ 25 · crash z ≥ 1.5 · **any single distress market ≥ 15%** (early-warning, capped here) |
| **Calm** | none of the above |

Why these three Stressed triggers are all "broad or confirmed" and none is a single market:

- **Blended gauge ≥ 55** — the gauge is the mean of six signal families (prediction
  markets, options skew, vol complex, credit, equity drawdown, macro & China). A high
  reading *is* broad stress by construction.
- **Crash-basket ≥ 40** — the crash basket is the equal-weight mean of the Bear **MKT**
  sleeve (3 markets: bubble-burst, OpenAI-distress, Anthropic-distress). It's an average,
  not one print.
- **Crash-basket ≥ 25 with z ≥ 2** — the level must be elevated *and* statistically
  confirmed by its own 90-day momentum. This is the "confirmed" path.

## The one-line change that does it

**Monitor (`polymarket-ai-index.html`, `computeRegime`)** — deleted the single line
`if(stackMax>=25) stressed.push(...)`. `stackMax` now only feeds the **Elevated**
single-signal trip (`stackMax ≥ 15`). Everything else was comment/label cleanup.

**Landing (`index.html`, `applyServerData`)** — removed `bubble>=25` from the Stressed
condition, so `regime="stressed"` now needs `sg.score>=55 || crashLevel>=40`. The
bubble-burst market (a single market) can still raise Elevated via the `bubble>=15` rule.
Driver-box copy updated to stop saying "≥25% = Stressed".

**Server/alerts (`update-market-data.py`, `compute_regime`)** — same removal of `bubble
>= 25` from the stressed branch. This function drives the **escalation push notifications**
(state in `alert-state.json`), so without this fix an alert could fire "STRESSED" on one
market spiking, contradicting both pages. **There are three copies of the regime rule and
they must stay in sync** — see the checklist below.

## Why the structural fix and not the China-specific one

Two options were on the table:

1. **Exclude China from the crash regime** — surgical, removes the specific wrong input.
2. **Cap any single-signal trip at Elevated** — structural, protects against *any* single
   market over-firing, not just China. **← chosen.**

Option 2 was chosen because the failure mode isn't unique to China: any single distress
market (a thin-liquidity bubble-burst print, one euphoria read, a lone recession market)
could have flipped the regime to red. The structural fix treats every market uniformly.

**Trade-off, stated plainly:** a single distress market — even the bubble-burst market —
now tops out at Elevated no matter how high it goes, until a broad or confirmed metric
agrees. If we later decide the bubble-burst market specifically *should* be able to reach
Stressed on its own (it is the most crash-relevant single market), that's a deliberate
carve-out to add back — but it reopens the "one market → red" door on purpose.

The complementary lever (option 1) is still available and independent: if China's
competition market shouldn't even count toward **Elevated** crash breadth, exclude it from
the `stack` items that feed `stackMax`. Not done here, per the "treat all markets
uniformly" choice.

## Keeping the three copies in sync (checklist for any future regime change)

The regime rule is duplicated in three places because each serves a different surface. Any
change to the thresholds or triggers must be applied to **all three**, or the surfaces will
contradict each other:

1. **`omen/polymarket-ai-index.html` → `computeRegime()`** — the monitor page chip. Has the
   most inputs (gauge, crash level, crash z-score, full distress stack).
2. **`omen/index.html` → `applyServerData()`** — the landing-page headline chip + ticker.
   Simpler inputs (server gauge from `market-data.json`, live crash level, bubble market).
   No z-score. Will be *no more* sensitive than the monitor by design.
3. **`omen/update-market-data.py` → `compute_regime()`** — server-side, drives escalation
   **push notifications** via `alert-state.json`. Inputs: gauge + MKT sleeve + bubble.

The invariant that ties them together: **no single market may produce a Stressed result in
any of the three.** Stressed comes only from the blended gauge or the crash-basket average
(optionally z-confirmed).
