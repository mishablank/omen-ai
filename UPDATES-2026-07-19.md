ranking NVDA against Apple by market cap says nothing about the AI trade. The

  (AVGO, ANET, SMCI); Power = the physical constraint (VST, CEG); Benchmarks

  *beating* NVDA over 3 months – the honest replacement for "how many are
  bigger".
- **Stat row**: NVDA 3M; customers / neoclouds / Micron 3M spread vs NVDA in
  points.
- **Group bars**: equal-weight 3M spread vs NVDA per group.
- **Table**: every name with 1M / 3M / 6M returns, 3M spread vs NVDA, 60-session
  correlation of daily returns with NVDA (ρ), and drawdown from the 6M high;
  NVDA pinned on top as the reference row.
- **Verdict**: names *every* divergence that fires (memory hotter than the GPU,

  not just the first match.
- **Polymarket crown demoted** to a context footnote ("a size ranking vs Apple

**Mechanics**: `stackRet()` returns null when a series covers <80% of the
window (short-history names must not masquerade as full-horizon returns);

## Market capitalization added to the stack card (same day, follow-up)

Mike clarified he wanted the *actual market caps* compared, not only relative
performance. The card now leads its data section with a **Market
capitalization (NVDA = 100%)** block:

- **Source**: `nasdaq_caps()` in `update-market-data.py` – one keyless request
  to Nasdaq's screener API (`api.nasdaq.com/api/screener/stocks?...&download=true`),
  which returns every US-listed common stock (NYSE + Nasdaq, incl. foreign
  private issuers IREN/NBIS) with marketCap + last sale. Chosen because
  Yahoo's quote endpoint is crumb-gated and SEC XBRL 404s on Alphabet's share
  classes and is annual-only for the foreign filers. Gzip handled explicitly

  forward with its original `asof`.

  (SOXX/SPY) excluded, they have no cap.
- **Card**: NVDA cap headline + ratio stats (= n× Micron, n× the 3 neoclouds
  combined, n% of the 5 customers combined), per-name bars scaled to
  NVDA = 100%, a Mkt cap column in the table, and a size sentence appended to
  the verdict. Caps missing (old JSON) → block hidden, card degrades to the
  performance view.
- **At ship**: NVDA $4.91T = 5.1 Microns ($959B), 51× the three neoclouds
  combined ($97B), 42% of its five biggest customers combined ($11.79T).

## Landing page rebuilt around a single verdict; index panels, gauge, and methodology moved to their own pages

**Why.** Mike flagged the landing page as carrying too much data – both index
panels, the crash-pressure gauge (rendered five separate times), the results
band, and the full methodology on one page – with no synthesis. A visitor had
to combine Bull 0.695 + Elevated themselves. The goal: an actionable answer at
the top of the page, with each data layer one click away.

**The verdict** (`omen/index.html`). A deterministic, published rule – no
judgment call between the data and the headline:

- **Direction** = the Bull side's pair share (bullish ≥ 55%, mixed 45–55%,
  bearish < 45%). Bull + Bear = 1, so the headline needs only one of them.
- **Stress** = the crash-pressure regime, same trip rules as the monitor
  (Stressed at gauge ≥ 55 or crash basket ≥ 40; Elevated at gauge ≥ 35, crash
  basket ≥ 25, or bubble-burst ≥ 15%; single-market trips capped at Elevated).
- **Matrix** direction × stress → five states: Risk-on / Constructive · hedged /
  No edge / Caution / Risk-off. The 3×3 table renders on the page with today's
  cell highlighted; `methodology.html#verdict` documents the thresholds. States
  are worded as what markets *price* – the hero carries the honesty caveat
  inline (no proven predictive edge, not investment advice).

**Page moves** – content moved, not deleted:

- Full Bull/Bear panels (constituents, sparklines) → new `omen/indexes.html`.
- Reading meter, five signal families, equity drawdown tiles, reference
  ranges → new `omen/gauge.html`.
- How-it-works, methodology i–v, verdict rule, run-it-yourself → new
  `omen/methodology.html`.
- Deleted outright: the Results stat band (all four numbers exist elsewhere)
  and the duplicate "Today's reading" section. Crash pressure now renders
  twice on the landing page (ticker + hero dial) instead of five times.
- Old anchors redirect: `index.html#indexes/#gauge/#method/#reading` →
  the new pages. `ai-capex.html` links updated.
- Fixed a pre-existing race: the dial's 0→baked counter animation could
  finish *after* the live gauge value landed and overwrite it (`dialSeq`
  guard in `setDial`).

**Tests.** `omen/test-verdict.mjs` slices the verdict block out of
`index.html` (same pattern as `test-pure-helpers.mjs`): all 9 matrix cells,
direction/regime thresholds, and a check that the published matrix markup
agrees with `verdictOf` cell-by-cell.
