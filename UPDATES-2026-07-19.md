# Updates – 2026-07-19

## "The #1 crown" card reworked into "NVDA vs the AI stack"

**Why.** Mike flagged the crown card as a totally wrong read of the AI stocks:
ranking NVDA against Apple by market cap says nothing about the AI trade. The
comparisons that do are inside the stack – hyperscalers, neoclouds (CRWV, IREN),
and DRAM producers (Micron).

**What the card now shows** (`omen/polymarket-ai-index.html`, same `p-crown`
card id and element hooks):

- **Peer groups** (`STACK_GROUPS`): Hyperscalers = the customers (MSFT, GOOGL,
  AMZN, META, ORCL); Neoclouds = the levered landlords (CRWV, NBIS, IREN);
  DRAM/HBM = the memory supplier (MU); Semis & networking = fellow suppliers
  (AVGO, ANET, SMCI); Power = the physical constraint (VST, CEG); Benchmarks
  (SOXX, SPY). All tickers already ship in `market-data.json`'s 6-month equity
  block – no new fetches, no fetcher changes.
- **Headline number**: how many AI-stack names (benchmarks excluded) are
  *beating* NVDA over 3 months – the honest replacement for "how many are
  bigger".
- **Stat row**: NVDA 3M; customers / neoclouds / Micron 3M spread vs NVDA in
  points.
- **Group bars**: equal-weight 3M spread vs NVDA per group.
- **Table**: every name with 1M / 3M / 6M returns, 3M spread vs NVDA, 60-session
  correlation of daily returns with NVDA (ρ), and drawdown from the 6M high;
  NVDA pinned on top as the reference row.
- **Verdict**: names *every* divergence that fires (memory hotter than the GPU,
  landlords marked down, arms dealer outrunning customers, …), biggest first –
  not just the first match.
- **Polymarket crown demoted** to a context footnote ("a size ranking vs Apple
  and friends, not a read on the AI stack"); `fetchCrown()` unchanged and still
  feeds it.

**Mechanics**: `stackRet()` returns null when a series covers <80% of the
window (short-history names must not masquerade as full-horizon returns);
`stackCorr()` date-aligns before correlating; `p-crown` added to
`FETCHER_PANELS` since the card now depends on the fetcher (stale badge +
"run the fetcher" empty state).

**Read at ship time** (equity through 2026-07-17): NVDA +2.2% over 3M, MU
+85.7% (+83pp vs NVDA – the HBM squeeze), neoclouds −22.5pp equal-weight
(CRWV −38.8%, IREN −29.5%), hyperscalers −9.8pp, SOXX +26.3pp. Three
divergences firing at once.

**Gotcha reconfirmed**: the browser pane screenshots this site solid black once
scrolled below the fold; verified via DOM reads + hiding preceding cards to
shoot the card at scroll 0.

## Market capitalization added to the stack card (same day, follow-up)

Mike clarified he wanted the *actual market caps* compared, not only relative
performance. The card now leads its data section with a **Market
capitalization (NVDA = 100%)** block:

- **Source**: `nasdaq_caps()` in `update-market-data.py` – one keyless request
  to Nasdaq's screener API (`api.nasdaq.com/api/screener/stocks?...&download=true`),
  which returns every US-listed common stock (NYSE + Nasdaq, incl. foreign
  private issuers IREN/NBIS) with marketCap + last sale. Chosen because
  Yahoo's quote endpoint is crumb-gated and SEC XBRL 404s on Alphabet's share
  classes and is annual-only for the foreign filers. Gzip handled explicitly
  (urllib doesn't auto-decompress). On failure the previous snapshot carries
  forward with its original `asof`.
- **JSON shape**: `caps: {asof, by: {SYM: {cap, px}}}` – ETF benchmarks
  (SOXX/SPY) excluded, they have no cap.
- **Card**: NVDA cap headline + ratio stats (= n× Micron, n× the 3 neoclouds
  combined, n% of the 5 customers combined), per-name bars scaled to
  NVDA = 100%, a Mkt cap column in the table, and a size sentence appended to
  the verdict. Caps missing (old JSON) → block hidden, card degrades to the
  performance view.
- **At ship**: NVDA $4.91T = 5.1 Microns ($959B), 51× the three neoclouds
  combined ($97B), 42% of its five biggest customers combined ($11.79T).

Caps go live on the next refresh.yml run (it has workflow_dispatch; triggered
