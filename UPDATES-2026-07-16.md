# OMEN – Pair Normalization: BULL + BEAR = 1

**Date:** 2026-07-16
**Files modified:** `omen/index.html`, `omen/polymarket-ai-index.html`
**Live site:** https://omen-ai.blankm.workers.dev/

## The rule

The two indexes (OMN-B / OMN-X) are presented as **one two-sided market**. Each side's
displayed price is its **share of the pair**, so the two prices always sum to exactly **1**:

```
bull_share = bull_raw / (bull_raw + bear_raw)
bear_share = bear_raw / (bull_raw + bear_raw)          # = 1 − bull_share
```

where `bull_raw` / `bear_raw` are the existing equal-weight means of each side's live
constituents (the `indexOf()` / `index_level()` math – unchanged).

**24h change** is computed in share space, not raw space: reconstruct yesterday's raw
levels (`raw − d1`), normalize those, and subtract. By construction the two deltas are
**exactly zero-sum** – BULL +2.2¢ forces BEAR −2.2¢ – which is how a real YES/NO pair
trades. Never display a raw-space delta next to a share-space price.

**History / sparklines** are normalized per time bucket: resample both sides over the
**same** common window first, then take `bull_t / (bull_t + bear_t)` at each bucket.
Normalizing two independently-windowed series is wrong – the shares won't sum to 1.

## What stays raw (deliberately)

Raw levels remain the **analytical basis**; normalization is presentation only:

- **`omen/snapshots.csv` and `omen/update-market-data.py`** – the pipeline stores raw
  levels only. Shares are always derived at render time. Do NOT write share columns
  into the CSV: raw is the single source of truth, and shares are recoverable from it
  at any date (`bull/(bull+bear)`), while the reverse is not true.
- **Crash-pressure gauge** – its bands (≥25 / ≥40 on the Bear MKT sleeve) are calibrated
  on raw levels. `applyServerData()` still reads `indexOf("bear","mkt")`.
- **Constituent tables** – each market row shows its own YES probability, unchanged.
- **Monitor page tiles (0–100)** – Bull/Bear sit next to China and Macro, which have no
  pair to normalize against; the four tiles must stay on one comparable scale. The
  7d/z-score stats and the net-sentiment tile also read raw history.

Reason for the split: the shares destroy one dimension of information (both sides rising
together – e.g. hot markets across the board – is invisible in share space). The pair
price answers "which story is winning"; the raw levels answer "how loud is each story".

## Applying it to any future surface

Any new surface that **prices the pair** (widgets, og-image text, alerts, a possible API)
must show shares, not raw levels:

1. Get both raw levels from the same snapshot (never mix timestamps).
2. Divide each by the sum; guard the sum-is-zero case.
3. Deltas and history: normalize per timestamp first, then difference – never
   normalize a raw delta.
4. Label it as a share ("bull + bear = 1"), not as "index level 0–100".

Surfaces that **analyze** a single side (gauge, sleeves, z-scores, lead-lag study) keep
reading raw levels from `snapshots.csv`.
